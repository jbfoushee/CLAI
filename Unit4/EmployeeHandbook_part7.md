## 8. Workplace Behavior and Conduct
### 8.1 Anti-Harassment Policy
TechCorp Solutions prohibits harassment of any kind. Harassment includes unwelcome conduct based on race, color, sex, gender, sexual orientation, religion, age (40+), disability, national origin, pregnancy, military status, or other protected characteristics.

**Sexual Harassment:** Unwelcome sexual advances, requests for sexual favors, sexual comments/jokes, inappropriate touching, or sexual images/gestures.

**Other Harassment:** Slurs, derogatory comments, offensive jokes, intimidation, hostile behavior, or exclusion based on protected characteristics.

**Reporting:** Report to your supervisor, any manager, HR (hr@techcorp.com, ext. 5500), or Ethics Hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX, anonymous option available). All complaints investigated promptly and confidentially.

**Consequences:** Violations may result in training, warnings, suspension, or termination. **Retaliation is strictly prohibited** and will result in discipline up to termination.

### 8.2 Anti-Discrimination Policy
TechCorp Solutions provides equal employment opportunities. We do not discriminate based on protected characteristics in hiring, compensation, promotions, training, or any employment decision.

**Reasonable Accommodation:** We provide accommodations for disabilities, religious beliefs, and pregnancy-related conditions. Submit requests to HR. We'll engage in an interactive process to identify effective accommodations.

**Reporting:** Report discrimination to your supervisor, HR, or Ethics Hotline (1-800-XXX-XXXX). All complaints investigated confidentially. Retaliation prohibited.

### 8.3 Workplace Violence Prevention
**Zero Tolerance:** No violence, threats, intimidation, or weapons on company property or at work events. Prohibited: physical violence, threats (direct or indirect), weapons (except authorized security), aggressive behavior, stalking, or conduct creating fear.

**Reporting Immediate Threats:** Call 911, alert security, evacuate if safe. **Non-Immediate Concerns:** Report to supervisor, Security (ext. 5911), HR (ext. 5500), or Ethics Hotline.

**Domestic Violence:** Employees experiencing domestic violence affecting workplace safety should inform HR confidentially. We'll develop safety plans and provide support.

**Consequences:** Violations result in immediate termination, law enforcement involvement, and possible prosecution.

### 8.4 Substance Abuse Policy
**Prohibited:** Using, possessing, distributing illegal drugs, or being under the influence of alcohol/drugs during work hours or on company property. Prescription medications that impair performance must be reported to supervisor/HR.

**Alcohol at Company Events:** Moderate consumption permitted at approved events. Must arrange safe transportation. All conduct policies still apply.

**Drug/Alcohol Testing:** Conducted for pre-employment (safety-sensitive positions), reasonable suspicion, post-accident, random (safety-sensitive roles), and return-to-work. Refusal treated as positive result.

**Consequences:** First violation requires substance abuse assessment/treatment and possible suspension. Second violation or serious violations (distribution, operating equipment while impaired, refusal to test) result in termination.

**Assistance:** Contact Employee Assistance Program (1-800-555-EAP1) for confidential help. Voluntary self-reporting before violations may protect employment.

## 9. Employee Relations
### 9.1 Open Door Policy
TechCorp Solutions maintains open communication. Employees may voice concerns to:
1. Immediate supervisor
2. Department manager/director
3. Human Resources
4. Senior leadership
5. Ethics Hotline: 1-800-XXX-XXXX (anonymous option available)

All discussions handled confidentially. No retaliation against employees using this policy in good faith.

### 9.2 Grievance Procedures
**Grievances:** Formal complaints about work-related issues, including policy violations, working conditions, unfair treatment, discrimination, performance evaluations, or compensation disputes.

**Informal Resolution:** Discuss with the person involved, your supervisor, or HR before filing formal grievance.

**Formal Process:**
1. **Submit Written Grievance to HR** (within 30 days): Include details, dates, individuals involved, witnesses, policy violated, and desired resolution
2. **Investigation** (5-10 days): HR investigates and interviews parties
3. **Initial Response** (10-15 days): Written findings and action
4. **Appeal** (optional): Submit appeal to CHRO within 10 days; final decision within 15 days

All grievances handled confidentially. No retaliation.

### 9.3 Conflict Resolution
Employees encouraged to resolve conflicts through:
1. **Direct Communication:** Discuss privately, focus on behaviors not personality, use "I" statements, seek common ground
2. **Mediation:** Voluntary process with neutral HR/mediator facilitating discussion
3. **Manager Intervention:** When conflict impacts performance or involves policy violations
4. **HR Involvement:** For harassment, discrimination, retaliation, or legal concerns

Address issues promptly. Focus on problems, not people. Maintain professionalism.

### 9.4 Disciplinary Actions
**Progressive Discipline:**
1. Verbal Warning/Coaching (informal)
2. Written Warning (formal, in personnel file)
3. Final Written Warning (last chance before termination)
4. Termination

**Immediate Termination** for serious violations: theft, violence, weapons possession, harassment, discrimination, substance abuse, falsification, gross insubordination, confidentiality breaches.

**Process:** Termination reviewed/approved by HR and management, employee notified in private meeting, final pay/benefits discussed, company property returned immediately, system access deactivated.
